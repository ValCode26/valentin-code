Monte Terminillo is a majestic mountain in the Apennine range of central Italy, standing at 2,217 meters (7,274 feet) above sea level. It is located in the Lazio region, about 20 km from Rieti and roughly 100 km from Rome. Because of its proximity to the capital, it’s often called “La Montagna di Roma” (The Mountain of Rome).

What Makes Monte Terminillo Special?>

  • Skiing & Winter Sports 🏂❄️ – It’s one of the most famous ski resorts in central Italy, with slopes for all levels, from beginners to experts. The area has ski lifts, cross-country trails, and off-piste opportunities.
  • Hiking & Climbing 🥾🏔️ – In summer, it turns into a paradise for trekkers and mountaineers, offering stunning trails like the Anello di Terminillo, which provides breathtaking panoramic views.
  • Cycling & Motorbiking 🚴🏍️ – The curvy mountain roads are a dream for cyclists and bikers, attracting enthusiasts looking for a challenging ride.
  • Spiritual & Historical Sites ⛪ – Nearby, you’ll find the Santuario di San Francesco in Greccio, where St. Francis of Assisi created the first nativity scene in history.
  • Monte Terminillo is a fantastic year-round destination for adventurers, nature lovers, and weekend travelers from Rome
